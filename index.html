Welcome to Memories of Colors

This site was built to practice HTML and CSS. At this early stage of my learning process, I am more focused on the layout and style of the webpage more than I am its content, as you will see when you come across some Latin filler text. That being said, I still wanted to add a little bit of me to the words.


I wanted the page I was working on to be colorful, so I made the site's theme all about colors. Focusing on Primary and Secondary. I take the color, talk about it for a minute, think back as far as I can to some sort of significance that color had on my childhood, then discuss my relationship with it now. And that's it.


Enjoy!

Primary Colors:

Yes, Primary Colors, the hardworking chefs of the color world. Forever performing the "yellow and blue makes green" gag at parties, even when they weren't even asked to. But everyone grins and bears it because they're like royalty. Oh man, when they get drunk, ugh, it's the worst. SO entitled. Anyway, ladies and gentlemen, I give you The Primary Colors:

BLUE

Ah, blue. The color of boys, according to people who put labels on colors. And while we're at it, I'd like to say that I don't agree with that type of gender labeling, thank you very much. My son loves pink, gets really charged by it. I think that's just fantastic. Yeah, little buddy, you pick that pink ice cream. It doesn't make you gay. Even if it did, I would support that 100% too. You're gold with me, kid (gold, oh man, another great color)!

Wait a minute, where were we?! Oh yes, the color blue. Boy, what a nice color, eh? Its shades can feel towering and bold, yet soft and soothing. It spans the heavens, and reaches deep depths below the sea. It describes a downtrodden emotion, and with that, a style of music.

My Memories: As a kid, I played two seasons of soccer with the blue team. There weren't any sponsors for that league, all the teams were recognized by their uniform colors. We were the Royal Blue Team, to be exact. Naturally, it replaced regular blue on my list of "favorite colors" at the time. "I will tell you my favorite colors, Timmy. I like green, orange and royal blue. It's the best blue, you know."

Nowadays, I find myself to be more of a navy blue guy. I like its richness, and to be honest, the way it compliments my eyes. It's the color I reach for first when dressing. "You're my boy, Blue!"


RED

Fiery, embarrassed, glowing, are just some of the adjectives associated with the color Red. It is passionate. If red were a person you hung out with, it'd probably be your out-on-the-town buddy. That person you call on when you feel like getting into a little mischief.

And how about red in nature, huh? So stark against the rest of the world around it. A flaming sunset on the beach, crimson apples glinting upon green leafed trees, the eerie sight of blood. Powerful.

My memories: Okay, it's another soccer team, but this team was my first team ever. Like my Royal Blue squad's league, teams were known by color. This time of my life, I was really into green. I wished so bad that I could be on the damn Green Team. It wasn't until I marched in the Memorial Day parade that year, and saw the photos of me and my awesome red team, did I come to appreciate the color. We looked fiery, a little embarrassed, but man were we glowing.

Today, I immediately think of tomato sauce, and how it winks at you under the cheese on a pizza slice. And now I'm hungry.
